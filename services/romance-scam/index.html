Romance Scams To Watch Out For

Catfishing

Catfishing is the act of creating a fake online identity to deceive someone into a romantic relationship, often by using attractive photos. These scammers infiltrate legitimate dating websites, establish trust with users, and then coerce them into sharing personal information or sending money. As per the Federal Trade Commission (FTC) report, scammers mostly used Instagram (29%) and Facebook (28%) to contact their victims in 2022. Losses to internet dating scams in the United States amounted to over $139 million in 2020. These numbers have climbed up further, but let’s make sure you don’t turn out to be one of them.

Investment Scam

Perpetrators pose as wealthy suitors and promote fake investment opportunities to online victims. They may even craft their entire profile around that luxury lifestyle and money mindset. Befriending or sparking a romantic affair with one of these fake profiles may be a poor decision. But what’s even more adverse is taking their word for investment advice and investing through their sources. You may actually be investing in an illegitimate platform or might just be handing your money to a fraud. Even if you were presented with a screenshot of bank records or profits made through their investment life, it still does not justify them as a reliable source. If you suspect any of the following, we’re just a ring away.

Military Love Scam

Scammers impersonate military personnel to gain trust, then fabricate emergencies to request money from their victims. Their profile picture may display a good-looking and unrealistic identity. With a heavy show of affection and attention, the deceptive person may try to get into the victim’s good side. They may even pretend to express their feelings at an earlier stage into the relationship. Anticipating a fraud may be the last thought on your mind as someone showers you with affection. These scams frequently end with the military personnel tricking people into sending them money. They may invent fake travel expenses for a promised romantic encounter. Only for you to be ghosted overtime.

Medical Emergency Scam

Online dating scammers pretend to have serious medical emergencies, creating a sense of urgency for their victims to send money. The storyline may differ from having a sick family member, personal accident, major theft, etc. With rising dating scams, making an online payment to someone you haven’t already met may set you up for some misery. As these schemes become more widespread and complex, the number of people falling for dating site scams continues to rise. In 2022, almost 70,000 individuals reported being victims of love scams, resulting in a combined loss of $1.3 billion. Don’t be an easy prey to an online partner trying their best to get a hold onto your money.

Understanding How Dating Scams Operate

Romance scams have been around since the beginning of relationships, and scammers have always found ways to take advantage of people by telling convincing stories. With the advent of online dating, these relationship scams have become more sophisticated and evolved into long-term cons to gain victims’ trust. Moreover, the coronavirus pandemic created an environment where relationship scammers could take advantage of vulnerable people who spent more time online due to restrictions on in-person meetings.

 The way these dating scams operate is such that they begin by connecting with you online, over a social media or dating site. Their profile may display an unrealistically charming person. They may be quick to confess romantic feelings and may refuse or delay face-to-face video chats or meet ups. Their motive may fundamentally be stealing sensitive information or making financial gains with whatsoever reasons that fit their play.

Romance scams can happen to anyone, but older individuals tend to suffer more significant financial losses. Scammers often target older people because they are more likely to have valuable assets, such as homes or retirement funds, which they can steal. Women make up approximately two-thirds of romance fraud victims, with an average age of 50.

Signs You're Dealing With a Romance Scam

Regardless of the technique used, listed below are some online dating scam indicators:

Communication from Strange Sources

Be careful when accepting friend requests on social media. Avoid accepting requests from strangers; only accept requests from people you know and trust to ensure your safety and privacy. Clicking links or downloading files from user bios or messages unrelated to you may lead to illegitimate addresses.

Flattering responses & behavior

Be wary of overly complimentary messages and paste the text into a search engine to check if the same words have been used on websites that expose romance scams. Take things slowly and ask your potential partner questions to watch out for inconsistencies that might reveal an impostor.

Requesting sensitive information

Be careful not to reveal too much personal information in your dating profile or to someone you’ve only chatted with online.

It is recommended that you use well-known and trustworthy dating websites. Additionally, it is important to keep all communication within the messaging service provided by the dating site.

A genuine crypto broker or brokerage firm will ensure that you have sufficient time to analyze and evaluate the business opportunity and make a well-informed decision.

In need of financial aid

Scammers flood dating apps and websites with bogus profiles, so don’t assume you’re secure just because you initiated contact. Finally, never send money or gift cards or disclose your bank details to someone you’ve only met online. Even if they make it seem like a crisis, know that your money may not be safe with them. Likewise avoid sending compromising photos to online strangers that could later be used for extortion.

How Can I Recognise The Red Flags Of A Romance Scammer?

Our experts have identified several warning signs that indicate romance scammers may target you. These signs include:

  • They claim to have fallen in love with you too quickly.
  • There is very little information about them available online.
  • They refuse to communicate with you over video chat.
  • Their contact details do not lead to a real person when investigated by a skip tracer, process server, or private investigator.
  • When you perform a reverse image search, other websites warn that they are a romance scammer.
  • They ask you to switch to a more private messaging platform, such as WhatsApp.
  • They cancel plans to meet you due to an emergency and then ask for your financial help.

Romance scammers use these tactics and others to take advantage of unsuspecting victims. Don’t let this happen to you! Contact our team today to conduct a comprehensive and effective private investigation into any potential romance scams.
